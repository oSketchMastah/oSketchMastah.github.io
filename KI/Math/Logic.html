Logic Interface

Welcome to the Logic Interface! This part of the website will aim to teach you logic, alongside various principles for the conduct of thought. Everyone can benefit from learning the basics of mathematical logic, whether you're in a university program that's making you take a logic course for your degree, or the average Joe who cares to partake in a spicy debate over lunch, understanding logic better will help prevent you from looking like a fool. I'll say that the practical examples that will be given of the concepts here will seem very simple (as most examples should aim to be), however by abstracting these examples, you will come to realize that there are universal laws that apply to all statements, and that you can use these laws to determine if a particular statement is true, or false, or even indeterminate, when given some set of axioms.
The word "axiom" may be new to you. axioms are a major component in a deductive system, they are statements that are deemed true for the purpose of deducing further information about a system and its constituents. It is worth having a semantic distinction between axioms and the statements that can be concluded from them.
