Logic Interface

Welcome to the Logic Interface! The goal here is to distill a sense of the nature of mathematical reasoning through a direct analysis of the reasoning process. Through this analysis, much will be covered in the following chapters:

  1. Laws of Thought: This section will focus on taking that thing average Joe calls "common sense" and upgrading it into what mathematicians call "logic". Here, you will learn how to realize fallacious arguments, as well as construct valid arguments. You will also learn how to communicate statements in terms of the symbols used in logic, which will allow us to be more specific in studying statements.
  2. Form and Meaning: This section will focus on the nature of syntactically and semantically oriented modes of deduction. If you don't have a clue what that means, this section might make your life easier.
  3. Memory and Heuristics: This section will focus on the nature of memory and information recall, with a focus on how we can naturally improve this process.
I recommend going through these sections in the order that they are displayed, top to bottom.
