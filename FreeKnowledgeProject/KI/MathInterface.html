Mathematics Knowledge Interface

Welcome, this interface is designed to guide you to the mathematics knowledge you desire, The "Navigate" tab (at the top of the screen) will drop down a list of links, each leading to different groups of knowledge. Here, I will describe our Philosophy in teaching mathematics in both the wider context as well as the layout of the mathematics portion of this website.
There's a fair bit of planning that went into the structure of the site from this point on, so the theme of your desired knowledge should make it easy to decide the path to take using the "Navigate" tab at the top of the screen. The following illustration shows the topics you'll find as links to the respective links of this website:

Logic and Proof : This portion is devoted to providing a foundation for clear thinking by providing you with just the knowledge that mathematicians use in their own practice of clear thinking. Collectively, you could think of what is taught here as being guidelines for rigorous thought, analysis, argument, and proof.

  • Laws of Thought: A conspectus of what must always be true in any conceivable universe... and our thoughts; Logic, the language of reason
  • Representation, Definition, and Interpretation: A look at all the wonderful ways information can describe, and be described.
  • Heuristics and Memory: A look at how we can hack our brains to do the unbelievable; memory recall methods and function

Algebras - Objects, Numbers, and Systems: This portion will teach you how algebra can be used to better understand mathematical systems by showing how algebra can be used to develop those mathematical systems from the ground up.

  • To be Whole - It's Natural: A look at how the concepts of addition and semantic aliasing can lead to algebra and the entire infinitum of whole numbers.
  • Let's Get Real, then Surreal - Embracing the Continuum: A look at how whole numbers can be extended into a continuum, and how it can be studied.
  • Adding Complexity - Complex Numbers and Function: A look at complex numbers and their use in science and pure math/ calculus.
  • Higher Dimensions - Linear Algebra from Scratch: A look at how larger structures can be described with 'larger' numbers; A Breakdown of Linear Algebra.
  • The Graph Structure - Topology and Abstraction: A look at how to think mathematically about any system of related things.

Calculus - Continuum Mechanics: This portion will teach you how to reason about objects existing on a continuum, primarily through employing ideas built up in the previous two major sections.

  • Definitions: Limits, Derivatives, Integrals and the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus: A thorough look at the definitions of the tools used in Calculus, and understanding the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (FTC). **Try and read this first and relate the definitions you learn here to the skills you learn in the later sections
  • Derivatives - A Guide to Measuring Change: A thorough look at how some added structure used to describe infinitessimal change allows us to measure rates of change whilst developing the technique of syntactic parsing.
  • Integrals - The Art of Continuous Sums: A thorough look at how one can express and evaluate summations over arbitrary regions in arbitrary numbers of dimension; practice in the art of creation.
